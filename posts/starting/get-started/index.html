Starting a blog

by Joy

2020-01-21

Hi, I am Joy and I am starting this blog. I plan to publish at least once a month. My posts will be mostly about interesting Mathematical problems. Sometimes, there will be some posts involving computing, expect heterogeneous computation topics like GPGPU. As inspired by Francis Bach, I plan to release every first Monday of the month. Overall, I hope this blog can be helpful.

1Why Math or why theoretical or why research?

This is actually a problem that I have been thinking ever since I read the paper \(A^{\ast}\) sampling – what is the use case of this sampling approach, or, “I've read it, now what”, which constantly frustrates me, like reading papers helps so little in my research. Because even now, I don't know when will I ever use this algorithm. In other words, I cannot justify spending time and energy reading such a paper, which talks about how one can perform exact sampling from Gibbs measure given any energy function. Sometimes, I feel stupid to spend huge amount of time on something that does not directly benefit me. It feels like I use this time for playing video games (or just have fun). But when I look back, this was never not my first “fail” investment, which becomes another hidden problem – why do I keep doing this?

As it turns out, Grant Sanderson has figured it out for me, at least partially. In his talk, titled “What Makes People Engage With Math?”, he talked about why the most viewed videos of his channel are not something as practical as “Nerual Network” or “Fourier Transform”. The reason behind this, and he made a brilliant analogy, “when reading Harry Potter, when will I ever use Wingardium Leviosa?”, is similar to the reason people read novel and fiction. “It's about emotion, it's about wonder, and it's about a mystery that you just need to see resolved.” This inspires me, working out Mathematical problems is not that much different from reading other entertaining materials. It just attracts you from another angle. Peeling off the obscure veil of Mathematical symbols, it just prompts you a question that you have to see resolved. The more peculiar the problem is, the more engagement one will put in.

In short, I choose this path mainly because I have fun in it. I hope that cruel reality won't rip this off me. I hope that, in the future, I can think more clearly and have more fun.
